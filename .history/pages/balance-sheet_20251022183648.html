    About the Balance Sheet Module
    Purpose

    The Balance Sheet module helps you measure your company’s financial position at a specific point in time. It summarizes what your business owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and retains (equity), offering a snapshot of stability and solvency.

    How It Works

    Use the AI Agent to generate or update your balance sheet by entering details about cash, receivables, debts, and investments. The AI can guide you through categorizing short-term and long-term assets and liabilities while calculating net equity automatically.

    Understanding the Sections
    • Assets: Everything your company owns—cash, inventory, property, and investments.
    • Liabilities: Debts, accounts payable, and other financial obligations.
    • Equity: The owner’s residual interest after liabilities are subtracted from assets.
    Why It Matters

    A well-maintained balance sheet reveals your business’s true financial standing. It helps lenders evaluate creditworthiness, investors assess value, and management make informed growth decisions.

    Best Practices
    • Update your balance sheet every quarter or month.
    • Ensure total assets equal total liabilities plus equity.
    • Use it alongside your income statement and cash flow report for full insights.
    • Leverage the AI Agent to identify strengths, weaknesses, and potential risks.
